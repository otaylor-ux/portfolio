§ 01 Approach

Empowering people with AI

A new AI tool launches every week, and chasing each one alone slows researchers down more than it helps. I lead the exploration — building custom tools, connecting our stack through APIs and AI workflows — and gave my team a structured way to learn and vet AI alongside me, so we move fast without losing the rigor real customer contact requires.

Build systems, not one-offs

A single great study helps one team once. A repository, an intake process, an impact framework — those compound. I've spent my career building the infrastructure that lets research scale past what any one researcher can carry.

Coach toward influence

Craft gets a study done. Storytelling and executive fluency get it acted on. I coach researchers to write compelling reports and hold their own in a room with the C-Suite

§ 02 Case Studies

TYPE Usability + generative research PARTNERS Product, Design, Engineering TIMEFRAME Pre-launch

Catching the Customer 360 funnel before it shipped

The team was moving toward implementation when our research surfaced major usability problems in the flow. Rather than filing a findings report, I partnered directly with Design and Product to rework the experience alongside them, in the room, before launch.

Impact → A substantially improved design shipped instead of the original — reducing risk and preventing what would likely have been significant customer friction at scale.

TYPE Research ops design SCOPE All product pillars TIMEFRAME Built & running since 2020

A two-track research program built for both speed and depth

Product pillars needed two different things from research at once: fast answers for ad-hoc questions, and deep strategic work for the big bets. I designed and launched a two-track program so the team could serve both without one crowding out the other.

Impact → Research went from a bottleneck teams routed around to a resource every pillar could reliably pull from — at close to 100 studies fielded a year.

TYPE Systems build SCOPE Org-wide TIMEFRAME Conceived & scaled in-house

A research repository that made past work findable

Teams were re-running studies that had already been done, because nothing linked existing insight to the roadmap items it should have informed. I conceived and built a searchable repository connecting recommendations directly to roadmaps and backlogs.

Impact → Cut duplicative research and made the org's accumulated knowledge something people actually used, not just archived.

TYPE Process + metrics design SCOPE 8-person team, dozens of stakeholders TIMEFRAME Iterated continuously

Moving the team from reactive requests to proactive priorities

I built and repeatedly iterated on an intake and prioritization system that balanced researcher capacity against business goals, then layered an impact-tracking framework on top — tying every study directly to OKRs and company "big bets," not just to the request that spawned it.

Impact → Shifted the team's work from answering whoever asked loudest to a transparent, business-aligned queue — and gave leadership a clear line from research to results.

TYPE Executive engagement FORUM Quarterly business reviews TIMEFRAME Ongoing, direct to CPO

Turning executive opinion into executive evidence

As the highest-ranking research leader in the company, reporting directly to the CPO, I presented insight in quarterly business reviews that shaped roadmap prioritization and company strategy — and organized executive immersion trips to build leadership's own firsthand connection to users.

Impact → Helped move company decision-making from assumption-driven to evidence-driven at the level where it matters most.

§ 04 AI-Native Research Practice

Research leadership is changing fast, and I'm building my team for where it's going rather than where it's been. I'm fluent with the CLI and with MCP servers, and I'm currently vetting and onboarding AI-native research tools into our stack. I've begun leading my own team through the adoption of AI into daily research workflows — not as a novelty layer on top of existing process, but as a genuine shift in how synthesis, recruitment, and analysis get done.

This is the same instinct behind everything else on this page: build the system before the org realizes it needs one.
